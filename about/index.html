About us

Raelize, a dedicated team of device security experts, was founded in 2020 by Cristofaro Mune and Niek Timmers. Drawing upon over a decade of collaborative research in device security, the co-founders established Raelize to leverage their combined expertise in a professional capacity.

We provide an offensive security perspective on device security through comprehensive training, expert consultancy, and rigorous testing services. Our approach is fundamentally research-driven, ensuring cutting-edge methodologies and insights inform all our offerings.

We are capable of addressing all aspects of device security, encompassing both hardware and software security. Rather than separating them, we believe in a holistic approach that considers the complete device. We partner with leading companies to enhance their device security posture throughout the device' lifecycle and elevate the expertise of their personnel through expert training programs.

Cristofaro Mune

Cristofaro brings over 20 years of expertise to the device security field. With a foundational background in physics, he possesses a unique ability to synthesize knowledge across multiple disciplines, delivering distinctive insights into complex areas such as Trusted Execution Environments (TEE) and Fault Injection (FI).

He shared his research among his peers at various security and academic conferences, such as Black Hat (2009, 2015), HITB (2018, 2021), hardwear.io (2017, 2020, 2022, 2025), Usenix WOOT (2024) and Langsec (2025).

He's on the review board for Usenix WOOT, HS3 workshop, hardwear.io and HackInBo.

As an experienced trainer, he is passionate about sharing his insights and discoveries with others, consistently demonstrating a commitment to knowledge transfer and professional development.

Niek Timmers

Niek brings over 10 years of expertise to the device security field. With a background in System and Network Engineering and an intrinsic interest, he's able to digest the complexities of device security efficiently.

He shared his research with the community at various security and academic conferences, as well as journals, such as Black Hat (2016, 2018, 2018), Bluehat (2019), Usenix WOOT (2024), hardwear.io (2017, 2020), FDTC (2016, 2017) and PoC||GTFO (2017, 2017).

As a seasoned trainer, he loves to share his experiences with others, always dedicated to providing a challenging and inspiring training.
